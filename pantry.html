Why eating expired food can be risky

“Best by,” “Use by,” and “Sell by” dates can mean different things. Some foods only lose flavor or texture after the date, while others can actually become unsafe to eat.

Here’s a quick overview of what tends to be high, medium, and low risk when eaten past the date:

High risk: fresh meat, poultry, seafood, deli foods, soft cheeses, leftovers. Risk: harmful bacteria or toxins can grow even if food looks fine. Watch for: slime, swelling packages, sour smell, visible mold.

Medium risk: canned/jarred goods that are dented, bulging, rusty, or very long past date. Risk: quality loss and possible toxin growth if the seal is compromised. Watch for: swelling, leaks, off smells/taste.

Low risk: dry goods like pasta, beans, rice, or unopened canned foods in good condition. Risk: flavor and nutrients fade more than safety—still check for moisture, pests, or odd smells.

When in doubt, throw it out. This list isn’t comprehensive—always research specific foods if you’re unsure.
